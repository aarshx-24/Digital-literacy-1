**Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud**

UPI/Online Payment Fraud is a common cybercrime in which fraudsters trick people into sending money or revealing banking details through digital payment apps. This crime usually happens when the criminal pretends to be a genuine buyer, seller, or service provider. For example, a college student named Rohan posted an advertisement online to sell his old phone. A scammer contacted him and claimed he would make the payment through UPI. First, the scammer sent a fake payment screenshot to create trust. Then, he asked Rohan to scan a QR code, saying it was required to receive money. In reality, the QR code generated a payment request. The scammer guided him on a phone call and asked him to enter his UPI PIN. As soon as Rohan entered the PIN, money was deducted from his account. Such frauds mainly target students, senior citizens, shopkeepers, and people who are not fully aware of digital payment systems. The consequences include financial loss, mental stress, panic, and possible misuse of bank details in the future. This case shows that users should never share OTPs, never enter their UPI PIN to receive money, and always verify payment requests carefully.
