Here is a **“Stay Safe Online” checklist** for **college students in India**:

**Do these:**

*   **Use strong, unique passwords** for email, college portals, banking apps, and social media. Do not reuse the same password everywhere.
*   **Turn on two-factor authentication (2FA)** wherever possible, especially for email, Instagram, WhatsApp, and banking apps. This adds an extra layer of protection.
*   **Keep your phone, laptop, browser, and apps updated** so known security flaws are patched quickly. The National Cyber Crime Portal advises following key cyber-safety practices to protect devices and data.
*   **Verify links before clicking**, especially if a message creates urgency such as “account blocked,” “scholarship approved,” or “parcel issue.” Phishing often starts with fake links and fake login pages.
*   **Check privacy settings on social media** and avoid posting personal details like address, ID cards, travel plans, or fee-payment screenshots. This reduces misuse of your identity and data.
*   **Avoid banking or UPI transactions on public or open Wi-Fi**, such as at cafés, stations, or hostels without trusted protection. RBI specifically warns against doing financial transactions on public/open/free Wi-Fi networks.

**UPI and money safety:**

*   **Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, CVV, card number, or bank password with anyone.** RBI explicitly warns not to share these details.
*   **Remember: UPI PIN is used to send money, not receive it.** If someone asks you to enter a UPI PIN to “get money,” it is a scam.
*   **Do not scan random QR codes sent by strangers to receive money, prizes, refunds, or part-time job payments.** NPCI warns that fraudsters may send links or QR codes and trick users into entering their UPI PIN.
*   **Double-check the receiver name and amount before approving any UPI payment** and review alerts immediately after a transaction.

**If something goes wrong:**

*   **Report financial cyber fraud immediately** at the **National Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in** or call the **helpline 1930** as soon as possible. The official portal specifically supports reporting cybercrime complaints, and it lists **1930** for online financial fraud reporting.
